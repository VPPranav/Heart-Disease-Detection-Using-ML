Learn about heart disease risk factors and prevention

Risk Factor Analysis

The chart above shows the relative importance of different factors in predicting heart disease. Age, cholesterol levels, and blood pressure are among the most significant indicators.

Blood Pressure

High blood pressure forces your heart to work harder to pump blood, which can lead to heart disease over time.

Tip: Aim for a blood pressure below 120/80 mm Hg.

Cholesterol

High cholesterol can lead to plaque buildup in your arteries, increasing your risk of heart disease and stroke.

Tip: Maintain total cholesterol below 200 mg/dL through diet and exercise.

Physical Activity

Regular exercise strengthens your heart and improves circulation, reducing your risk of heart disease.

Tip: Aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate activity per week.

Smoking

Smoking damages blood vessels and reduces oxygen in the blood, significantly increasing heart disease risk.

Tip: Quitting smoking can reduce your heart disease risk by up to 50% within one year.

Prevention Strategies

1

Healthy Diet

Eat a diet rich in fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and lean proteins. Limit saturated fats, trans fats, sodium, and added sugars.

2

Regular Exercise

Engage in moderate aerobic activity for at least 150 minutes per week, or 75 minutes of vigorous activity.

3

Manage Stress

Practice stress-reduction techniques such as meditation, deep breathing, or yoga to lower your heart disease risk.

4

Regular Check-ups

Visit your healthcare provider regularly to monitor your blood pressure, cholesterol, and other risk factors.
